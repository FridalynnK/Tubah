❤️ Health Sector

Healthcare in Tubah

An overview of health infrastructure, common health challenges, and available medical services across the subdivision.

🏥

Health Facilities

Tubah has a District Hospital in Nkwen and several integrated health centres across villages including Bambili, Bambui, and Kejom Keku.

12 Facilities 3 Under Renovation
🤒

Prevalent Conditions

Malaria remains the leading cause of outpatient visits. Respiratory infections, typhoid, and maternal complications are also commonly reported challenges.

Malaria #1 Burden
👶

Maternal & Child Health

Antenatal care coverage has improved with community health workers deployed in remote villages. Neonatal mortality reduction remains a priority.

ANC Coverage Rising
💊

Drug Supply & Shortages

Periodic stock-outs of essential medicines affect service delivery. Community pharmacies partially bridge gaps but accessibility is uneven in rural areas.

Intermittent Shortages
🌿 Agriculture

Agriculture & Food Security

Tubah's fertile highlands support diverse farming. Key crops and livestock sectors drive the local economy.

🥬

Horticulture

Irish potatoes, cabbages, carrots, and leafy vegetables thrive in the cool highland climate. Bambili and Bambui are key production zones supplying Bamenda markets.

☕

Coffee & Cash Crops

Arabica coffee cultivation has deep historical roots. Arabica cooperatives operate in parts of the subdivision, though output has declined over recent years.

🐄

Livestock Farming

Cattle rearing by Mbororo pastoralists coexists alongside smallholder farming. Tensions over grazing corridors occasionally arise, requiring mediation.

🌱

Extension Services

Government and NGO agricultural extension officers offer training on improved seeds, soil management, and post-harvest handling to reduce losses.

⚖️ Land Disputes

Land Affairs & Disputes

Land tenure conflicts are a recurring challenge in Tubah. Below is an overview of key dispute categories and resolution mechanisms.

14

Boundary Conflicts

Inter-village and family-level boundary disputes are the most common, often involving inherited land with no formal documentation.

High Frequency
06

Farmer–Grazier Disputes

Conflicts between crop farmers and Fulani cattle herders over land use and crop destruction are regularly mediated by local chiefs.

Seasonal
03

State Land Disputes

Disputes involving government-allocated land and community claims, particularly near university lands in Bambili, are pending legal resolution.

Pending Court
⚠️

Resolution Pathways: Most land disputes in Tubah are handled through traditional councils and customary law before escalating to civil courts. The Sub-Divisional Officer (SDO) plays a key mediation role. Community members are encouraged to register land titles through the Divisional Delegation of Land Affairs to reduce future conflicts.

🎓 Education Sector

Higher Education in Tubah

Tubah Subdivision is home to two major national tertiary institutions, making it an important academic hub in the North West Region.

🏛️

University of Bamenda

📍 Bambili, Tubah Subdivision

Established in 2010, the University of Bamenda (UBa) is a state university serving the North West Region of Cameroon. Its main campus is located in Bambili, within Tubah Subdivision, and hosts several faculties and higher technical institutes.

📅Founded 2010
🏫Main campus in Bambili
👨‍🎓Serves students from across the North West Region
🇨🇲Government-funded state university
Key Faculties & Schools

⚙️

National Polytechnic Institute Bamenda

📍 Bambui, Tubah Subdivision

The National Polytechnic Institute Bamenda (POLYBAM), located in Bambui, is a public professional institution under the University of Bamenda. It focuses on practical, technical, and vocational training to develop skilled professionals for Cameroon's workforce.

🏫Located in Bambui
🔧Focus on technical & vocational training
🏛️Under the University of Bamenda
💼Trains graduates for industry & public sector
Training Departments
Civil Engineering Electrical Engineering Computer Science Mechanical Engineering Business & Management
📚

Education & Community: The presence of these two institutions has significantly shaped Tubah's economy and population. Bambili and Bambui host thousands of students and staff annually, driving demand for housing, transport, and local services. Community members are encouraged to engage with outreach programmes run by both institutions, and to report education-related infrastructure needs through the observation form below.
